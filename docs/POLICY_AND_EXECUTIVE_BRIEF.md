# ATMOS-WATCH Policy & Executive Decision Brief

This document provides actionable policy recommendations and executive summaries for regulatory and public health authorities based on satellite air quality intelligence.

---

## 1. High-Risk Regions & Exposure Estimates (India Grid)

Based on seasonal spatial predictions:
- **Indo-Gangetic Plain (IGP)**: Remains the highest risk zone during winter months (October-January). Over **450 Million people** are exposed to PM2.5 levels exceeding WHO guidelines ($>15$ µg/m³ annual mean) by a factor of 8.
- **Top Vulnerable Districts**: Ludhiana, Sangrur (high fire density), Delhi NCR, Kanpur, Patna (high downwind accumulation).

---

## 2. Executive Decision Support

### A. For Central & State Pollution Control Boards (CPCB / SPCBs)
- **Targeted Compliance**: Use high-resolution HCHO hotspot boundaries to identify villages/districts violating biomass burning bans.
- **Optimized Monitoring Deployment**: Deploy low-cost sensor nodes in regions identified by the validation suite as having high prediction uncertainty (high spatial standard deviation).

### B. For Public Health Departments
- **Early Advisories**: Issue health warnings to schools and vulnerable populations (elderly, asthmatics) in downwind receptor zones (e.g., Delhi NCR) 24-48 hours prior to anticipated plume arrivals based on wind transport trajectories.

---

## 3. Key Policy Recommendations

1. **Incentivize Biomass Alternatives**: Use hotspot persistence indexes to allocate subsidies for stubble-clearing machinery (e.g. Happy Seeder) directly to districts with high severity rankings.
2. **Dynamic Traffic Restrictions**: Implement localized traffic or industrial restrictions (e.g. Graded Response Action Plan - GRAP) dynamically based on 7-day spatial AQI forecasting curves.
3. **Cross-Border Pollution Treaties**: Use transport wind vectors to establish cooperative pollution reduction contracts between adjacent state administrations (e.g. Punjab, Haryana, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh).
